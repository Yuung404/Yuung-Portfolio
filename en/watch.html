// 04 — TECH INTELLIGENCE

TECH WATCH

Regular monitoring of cybersecurity news, networks and emerging technologies — sources: ANSSI, CERT-FR, The Hacker News, LeMagIT, Krebs on Security.

// JANUARY 2025

THE RISE OF RANSOMWARE AS A SERVICE (RAAS)

// SOURCE: ANSSI — Ransomware Threat Report 2024

Cybercriminal groups now offer their attack tools as a "service" (RaaS), dramatically lowering the barrier to entry for attackers. ANSSI recorded a 30% increase in ransomware incidents in France in 2024. A major challenge for SMBs and local authorities, which are often less well-protected than large enterprises.

// FEBRUARY 2025

CVE MANAGEMENT: THE IMPORTANCE OF PATCH MANAGEMENT

// SOURCE: CERT-FR — Security Bulletins

The majority of successful cyberattacks exploit known (CVE) unpatched vulnerabilities. CERT-FR regularly publishes security bulletins detailing critical flaws. Implementing a rigorous patch management process is a fundamental pillar of defence-in-depth for any organisation.

// FEBRUARY 2025

PHISHING & SOCIAL ENGINEERING: THE HUMAN FACTOR

// SOURCE: The Hacker News

Social engineering remains the #1 attack vector according to multiple studies. Spear-phishing techniques targeting employees with personalised, convincing messages often bypass technical defences. User awareness is as important as security tools for limiting the attack surface.

// MARCH 2025

SIEM AND SOC: REAL-TIME THREAT DETECTION

// SOURCE: LeMagIT — Cybersecurity Feature

Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) systems centralise and correlate security logs for real-time incident detection. Combined with a SOC (Security Operations Center), they enable rapid incident response. Leading tools: Splunk, Microsoft Sentinel, IBM QRadar. A key skill for future cybersecurity analysts.

// MARCH 2025

GENERATIVE AI AND CYBERSECURITY: A DOUBLE-EDGED SWORD

// SOURCE: Krebs on Security

Generative AI is being exploited on both sides: attackers use it to craft polymorphic malware and hyper-targeted phishing campaigns, while defenders leverage it for anomaly detection and automated incident response. Understanding these uses is essential to anticipate tomorrow's threats.

// APRIL 2025

NIS2 DIRECTIVE: NEW CYBERSECURITY OBLIGATIONS IN EUROPE

// SOURCE: ANSSI — NIS2 Transposition

The European NIS2 directive, transposed into French law, extends cybersecurity obligations to thousands of additional entities (health, energy, transport, public administration). Affected organisations must now report incidents within 24 hours, implement risk management measures and conduct regular audits. Major impact on IS security practices across sectors.
