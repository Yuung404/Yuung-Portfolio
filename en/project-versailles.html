CONTEXT

Versailles City Hall, located at 4 avenue de Paris in the Yvelines department, launched a broad initiative to modernise its digital infrastructure. The IT Department (DSI) was managing incidents through entirely informal channels: direct phone calls, WhatsApp messages and Teams chats. This approach produced critical failure points:

▸ Information loss — missed calls and messages during peak activity, leading to frustration among municipal staff
▸ No traceability — no centralised data, making it impossible to measure Helpdesk workload or calculate TTO/TTR resolution times
▸ Technical debt — without a rigorous inventory, licence tracking, critical updates and end-of-life OS mapping were uncontrolled

To address these challenges, the city decided to deploy GLPI (Gestionnaire Libre de Parc Informatique), an open-source ITSM solution proven across both public and private sector environments.

MISSION DESCRIPTION

Since 09/03/2026, I have been working as a support technician within the Versailles City Hall IT department. The mission, assigned by M. LATOUR (IT Director), covers four areas:

1. Needs assessment & solution selection
Analysis of the DSI's requirements and justification of GLPI as the appropriate open-source solution for the public sector.

2. Initial IT asset inventory
Identification and cataloguing of all equipment (workstations, servers, printers, etc.) and software across municipal services — inventory carried out both manually and automatically via the GLPI agent.

3. GLPI installation & configuration
Deployment of GLPI on a Debian 13 (Trixie) virtual machine with LAMP stack (Linux, Apache, MariaDB, PHP). Configuration of SLAs (Service Level Agreements), OLAs (Operational Level Agreements), escalation levels and ticket priority rules.

4. Team training
Training supervisory staff on the ticket management module, then rolling out access to all municipal agents.

TECHNICAL ARCHITECTURE

VM Server — Debian 13 "Trixie" virtual machine running on a Type 2 hypervisor (VirtualBox)

LAMP Stack — Linux (Debian 13) · Apache · MariaDB · PHP

GLPI v10.x — Web-based management interface: tickets, inventory, users, reports, SLA configuration

SLA / OLA — Configuration of maximum take-over times (TTO) and resolution times (TTR) with automatic escalation levels

EXPECTED OUTCOMES (Review — 29/12/2026)

▸ Centralisation — unified real-time management of the city hall's entire IT estate
▸ Cost reduction — better asset lifecycle management and licence optimisation
▸ Helpdesk efficiency — reduced TTO/TTR times through the ticketing system and automatic incident prioritisation
▸ Controlled inventory — complete, up-to-date mapping of hardware and software assets
▸ Automation — automated ticket handling, escalations and notifications
